Bioactive vivaria

I've had snakes on and off since about 2014 and one of the things that I've always wanted to do is to build a large enclosure for them to freely move around to better accomodate humidity and temperature preferences. In the fall of 2015, I began building a 450 gallon vivarium. I planted the upper terrestrial portion in February of 2017 and the bottom in May. As of my last count I have added over 28 species of plants, as well as lady bugs (to eat aphids etc), earth worms (to aerate soil), springtails (to eat fungi), isopods (two species to help break down decomposing matter), and flightless Drosophila spp (also part of the clean-up crew). I'm allowing the viv to cycle a bit longer before I add the snakes (it's finally starting to produce mold and fungus). Hopefully this will also allow the plants to grow in better before the snakes start crawling all over them.

I'm hoping to post more of the photos of the process, but given how long it's taken me to build the cage it may be a while. Lights are from JungleHobbies, misting system from MistKing.
